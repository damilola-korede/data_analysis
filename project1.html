Case Study: Clustering Small Business Owners Using K-Means

Objective

This project aimed to analyze the financial profiles of small business owners and non-business owners, identify patterns, and create clusters of small business owners with similar financial characteristics. The study involved exploratory data analysis, dimensionality reduction, and clustering using machine learning techniques.

Dataset

The data contained demographic and financial information, including variables like income, net worth, assets, and debts. Key data preprocessing steps included handling missing values, filtering data, and scaling numerical features.

Key Steps : Exploratory Data Analysis

Explored income distribution and categorized income ranges.

Visualized differences in household debt and home values between business and non-business owners.

Analyzed age distribution among small business owners.

Insights from EDA

The provided visualizations collectively offer key insights into the dynamics of business ownership, income distribution, household finances, and demographics. The first chart reveals significant disparity in income distribution, with business owners overwhelmingly dominating the highest income category, while non-business owners are prevalent in lower income categories. The second plot shows the relationship between home value and household debt, where most households are clustered in the lower ranges of both variables, indicating modest home values and debt levels for the majority. However, a few outliers with high home values and debt suggest financial behaviors or wealth disparities in specific households. The third chart illustrates the age distribution of small business owners, showing that the majority are between 40 and 60 years old, peaking around age 50. This suggests that mid-career individuals, likely due to accumulated experience and financial resources, are most inclined to own businesses, with significantly fewer younger owners and a decline in ownership among individuals over 70 years old.

Model Building: Feature Engineering

Focused on high-variance features to ensure the clustering model captured significant differences. Used trimmed variance to mitigate the impact of outliers.

Clustering

Tested K-Means clustering with varying cluster numbers (2-12) to find the optimal model using metrics like inertia and silhouette scores. Selected three clusters as the optimal solution, based on both metrics.

Line plot of inertia_errors vs n_clusters.

Line plot of silhouette score vs n_clusters.

Model Results

Clusters differentiated small business owners by their financial profiles, such as asset values and net worth.

Cluster Analysis

Created a bar chart to compare the mean values of financial features across the three clusters. Each cluster represented a distinct group of small business owners based on financial strength and stability.

Dimensionality Reduction

Applied Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to reduce the dataset's dimensionality to two components for better visualization. Visualized clusters in a two-dimensional space to validate separability.

PCA Visualization

A scatter plot displayed the three clusters in a two-dimensional space.
