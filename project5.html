01 — Overview
Objective

In many retail businesses, large volumes of sales data exist, but decision-makers still struggle to answer simple questions: Are we truly profitable? Which products and suppliers drive value? When do we actually make money?

This project was built to bridge that gap. Using Power BI, raw transactional sales data was transformed into a clear, executive-ready dashboard that tells the story of revenue growth, cost control, and profitability across products, categories, and suppliers.

04 — Methodology
Building Meaningful Metrics with DAX

To move beyond raw numbers, core business metrics were created using DAX. These measures form the backbone of the dashboard and ensure all visuals remain consistent and accurate as filters are applied.

Total Revenue:

Total_revenue = SUMX(Sheet1, Sheet1[Unit Price] * Sheet1[Quantity])

Total Cost:

Total_cost = SUMX(Sheet1, RELATED('Sheet1 (2)'[Cost Price]) * Sheet1[Quantity])

Profit:

Profit = [Total_revenue] - [Total_cost]

Profit Margin:

Profit_margin = DIVIDE([Profit], [Total_revenue])

These measures automatically respond to slicers (date, category, supplier), allowing stakeholders to view profitability from multiple angles without recalculating figures manually.

06 — Insights
Key Findings
  • Electronics dominate revenue — followed by home appliances; accessories contribute a smaller share, highlighting clear investment priorities.
  • Supplier profitability varies significantly — a small number of suppliers are clear top profit drivers, supporting data-backed procurement negotiations.
  • Revenue fluctuates over time — clear spikes and dips suggest seasonal patterns, promotional impacts, or supply constraints worth investigating.
  • 36% overall profit margin — a healthy baseline that can be improved by focusing on high-margin categories and optimizing supplier mix.
07 — Conclusion

By combining thoughtful data modeling, well-defined DAX measures, and intuitive visuals, this dashboard provides actionable insights that directly support profitability and strategic planning. Decision-makers can now interact with the data, apply filters, and get instant answers — without needing a data analyst present.
