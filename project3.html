Apartment Initial Pricing Analysis and Prediction

Objective

The project aimed to analyze the initial costs of apartments across different boroughs, identify trends, and predict future values using regression models.

Dataset

Utilized a dataset (obtained from kaggle website) containing apartment intial pricing, borough information, and related attributes. Preprocessed the data by removing irrelevant columns (e.g., "Horizontal Enlargement") and handling missing values.

Exploratory Data Analysis Insight

The analysis of apartment data across Brooklyn, Staten Island, and Queens reveals notable differences in price and representation. Queens stands out with the highest initial apartment prices, nearing 1 million dollars, while Staten Island shows moderate prices and Brooklyn has the lowest. In terms of representation, Staten Island dominates with the most apartments listed, followed by Queens, and Brooklyn with the least. Price distribution highlights a significant gap, as most apartments are priced around $100,000, with a smaller cluster at $500,000 and none in between, suggesting distinct market segments or a potential absence of data for intermediate price ranges.

Model Building

Built a Ridge regression pipeline: One-hot encoding of categorical features. Imputation for missing values. Baseline model comparison using Mean Absolute Error (MAE).
